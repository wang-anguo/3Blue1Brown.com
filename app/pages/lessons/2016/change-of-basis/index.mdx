> Mathematics is the art of giving the same name to different things.  
 数学是为不同的事物赋予相同名称的艺术。
> 
> \- Henri Poincaré. 亨利·庞加莱

If we have a vector sitting here in 2d space, we have a standard way to describe it with coordinates. In this case, the vector has coordinates $\left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 2  \end{array}\right]$, which means going from its tail to its tip involves moving $3$ units to the right, and $2$ units up.  
如果在二维空间中有一个向量，我们有一种标准的方法用坐标来描述它。  
在这个例子中，该向量的坐标为$\left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 2\end{array}\right]$，  
这意味着从向量的起点到终点需要向右移动3个单位，再向上移动2个单位。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/introduction/VectorCoordinates.light.svg" />

Now, the more linear-algebra oriented way to describe coordinates is to think of each of those numbers as a scalar, a thing that stretches or squishes vectors. You think of the first coordinate as scaling $\hat{\imath}$, the vector with length one pointing to the right, while the second coordinate scales $\hat{\jmath}$, the vector with length one pointing straight up. The tip-to-tail sum of these two scaled vectors is what the coordinates are meant to describe.  
现在，从线性代数的角度来描述坐标的话，  
可以将这些数字视为标量——即能够拉伸或压缩向量的量。  
第一个坐标用于缩放指向右侧、长度为1的向量$\hat{\imath}$，  
而第二个坐标则用于缩放垂直向上、长度为1的向量$\hat{\jmath}$。  
这两个经过缩放后的向量首尾相加的结果，就是坐标所描述的含义。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/introduction/VectorCoordinatesBasis.light.svg" />

You can think of these two special vectors as encapsulating all the implicit assumptions of our coordinate system: the fact that the first number indicates rightward motion, that the second indicates upward motion, exactly how far a unit of distance is; all of that is tied up in the choice of $\hat{\imath}$ and $\hat{\jmath}$ as the vectors which our scalar coordinates are meant scale. The specific way that we translate a vector into a pair of numbers is called a "coordinate system", and these two special vectors $\hat{\imath}$ and $\hat{\jmath}$ are called the "basis" vectors of standard coordinate system.  
你可以将这两个特殊向量视为蕴含了我们坐标系所有隐含假设的载体：  
第一个数表示向右运动，第二个数表示向上运动，以及单位距离的具体长度；  
所有这些都与选择 $\hat{\imath}$ 和 $\hat{\jmath}$ 作为标量坐标所作用的向量密切相关。  
我们将向量转换为一对数值的具体方式被称为“坐标系”​，  
而这两个特殊向量 $\hat{\imath}$ 和 $\hat{\jmath}$ 则被称为标准坐标系的“基向量”​。

## Alternate system 其他坐标系

What we'd like to talk about here is the idea of using a different set of basis vectors. For example, let's say you have a friend, Jennifer, who uses a different set of basis vectors, which I'll call $\vec{\mathbf{b}_1}$ and $\vec{\mathbf{b}_2}$. Her first basis vector, $\vec{\mathbf{b}_1}$, points to the right and up a bit, and her second basis vector, $\vec{\mathbf{b}_2}$, points left and up.  
我们在这里想要讨论的是使用另一组基向量的想法。  
例如，假设你有一个朋友詹妮弗，她使用另一组基向量，  
我将其称为 $\vec{\mathbf{b}_1}$ 和 $   \vec{\mathbf{b}_2}$ 。  
她的第一个基向量 $\vec{\mathbf{b}_1}$ 指向右侧并略微向上，而  
她的第二个基向量 $\vec{\mathbf{b}_2}$ 则指向左侧并向上。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/alternate-system/AlternativeBasisVectors.light.svg" />

Now take another look at the vector we showed earlier, the one we would describe with coordinates $\left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]$ using our basis vectors $\hat{\imath}$ and $\hat{\jmath}$.  
现在再来看看我们之前展示的向量，  
即用基向量 $\hat{\imath}$ 和 $\hat{\jmath}$ 表示、坐标为 $\left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 2\end{array}\right]$ 的那个向量。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/alternate-system/VectorCoordinatesOriginal.light.svg" />

Jennifer would actually describe this vector with coordinates$\left[\begin{array}{c} 5/3 \\ 1/3 \end{array}\right]$. What this means is that the particular way to get that vector using her two basis vectors is to scale $\vec{\mathbf{b}_1}$ by $5/3$, scale $\vec{\mathbf{b}}_2$ by $1/3$, then to add them together.  
詹妮弗实际上会用坐标$\left[\begin{array}{c} 5/3 \\ 1/3\end{array}\right]$来描述这个向量。  
这意味着，使用她的两个基向量得到该向量的具体方法是：  
将 $\vec{\mathbf{b}_1}$ 乘以 $5/3$ ，将 $   \vec{\mathbf{b}}_2$ 乘以 $1/3$ ，
然后再将两者相加。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/alternate-system/VectorCoordinatesAlternative.light.svg" />

In a little bit we'll show you how you can figure out those two numbers. In general, whenever Jennifer uses coordinates to describe a vector, she thinks of the first coordinate as scaling $\vec{\mathbf{b}}_1$, and the second coordinate as scaling $\vec{\mathbf{b}}_2$, and she adds the results.  
稍后我们会向您展示如何求出这两个数值。  
一般来说，每当詹妮弗用坐标来描述一个向量时，她会  
将第一个坐标视为对 $\vec{\mathbf{b}}_1$ 的缩放，  
将第二个坐标视为对$\vec{\mathbf{b}}_2$的缩放，然后把结果相加。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/alternate-system/AlternativeVectorCoordinates.light.svg" />

What she gets will typically be completely different from the vector we think of as having those coordinates.  
她得到的结果通常会与我们所认为的具有那些坐标的向量完全不同。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/alternate-system/AlternativeVectorCoordinatesOriginal.light.svg" />

To be a little more precise, her first basis vector, $\vec{\mathbf{b}}_1$, is something we would describe with coordinates $\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]$, and her second basis vector, $\vec{\mathbf{b}}_2$, is something we would describe as $\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]$.  
更准确地说，  
她的第一个基向量 $\vec{\mathbf{b}}_1$ 可以用坐标 $\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]$ 来表示，而  
她的第二个基向量 $\vec{\mathbf{b}}_2$ 则可以用 $\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 1\end{array}\right]$ 来表示。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/alternate-system/AlternativeBasisVectorsSpecific.light.svg" />

It's important to realize that from her perspective, in her system, those vectors have coordinates $\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]$ and $\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]$. They are what define the meaning of the coordinates $\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]$ and $\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]$ in her world.  
必须认识到，从她的视角来看，在她所采用的坐标系中，  
这些向量的坐标分别为 $\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]$ 和 $\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]$。  
正是它们定义了坐标 $\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0\end{array}\right]$ 和 $\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]$ 在她所处世界中的含义。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/alternate-system/AlternativeCoordinateSystem.light.svg" />

So in effect, we are speaking different languages. We're all looking at the same vectors in space, but Jennifer uses different words and numbers to describe them.  
所以实际上，我们是在用不同的语言交流。  
我们都在观察空间中的相同向量，但詹妮弗用不同的词汇和数字来描述它们。

## The grid   网格

One quick word on how we're representing things here: When illustrating 2d space, we typically use a square grid. But this grid is just a construct, a way to visualize our coordinate system, so it depends on our choice of basis vectors. Space itself has no intrinsic grid.  
简单说明一下我们在这里如何表示事物：  
在描绘二维空间时，我们通常使用正方形网格。  
但这种网格只是一种构想，一种可视化坐标系的方式，因此它取决于我们所选择的基向量。  
空间本身并没有固有的网格结构。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/the-grid/SquareGrid.light.svg" />

Jennifer might draw her own grid, which would be an equally made up construct, meant as nothing more than a visual tool to help follow what her coordinates mean.  
詹妮弗可能会画出属于自己的坐标网格，那同样是一种人为构造的体系，  
只不过是一种视觉工具，用以帮助理解坐标所代表的含义。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/the-grid/AlternativeGrid.light.svg" />

Her origin would line up with ours, since everyone agrees what the coordinates $\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]$ should mean: It's the thing you get when you scale any vector by $0$.  
她的原点会与我们的对齐，因为大家都认同坐标 $\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 0\end{array}\right]$ 的含义：  
它是将任意向量乘以$0$后得到的结果。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/the-grid/GridOrigin.light.svg" />

The direction of her axes and the spacing of her grid lines will be different, depending on her choice of basis vectors.  
根据其所选基向量的不同，其轴的方向以及网格线的间距也会有所差异。

## Change of basis matrix 基变换矩

A fairly natural question to ask at this point is how we translate between coordinate systems. For example, if Jennifer describes a vector as $\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]$, what would its coordinates be in our system. How do you translate from her language to ours?  
此时一个很自然的问题就是如何在不同坐标系之间进行转换。  
例如，如果詹妮弗将一个向量描述为 $\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]$ ，  
那么它在我们的坐标系中的坐标会是多少？  
该如何将她所用的表示方式转换为我们的表示方式呢？

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/change-of-basis-matrix/TranslateHersToOurs.light.svg" />

Well, what her coordinates are saying is that this vector is $-1 \vec{\mathbf{b}}_1 + 2 \vec{\mathbf{b}}_2$. From our perspective, $\vec{\mathbf{b}}_1$ has coordinates $\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]$, and $\vec{\mathbf{b}}_2$ has coordinates $\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]$. When we scale and add these representations of her basis vectors in our coordinates, we get $\left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]$. So that's how we would describe the vector she thinks of as $\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]$.  
嗯，根据她的坐标来看，这个向量是 $-1 \vec{\mathbf{b}}_1 + 2\vec{\mathbf{b}}_2$ 。  
从我们的视角来看， $\vec{\mathbf{b}}_1$ 的坐标为 $\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]$ ，而 $\vec{\mathbf{b}}_2$ 的坐标为 $\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]$ 。  
当我们将这些基向量的表示按坐标进行缩放并相加后，得到的结果是$\left[\begin{array}{c} -4 \\1 \end{array}\right]$ 。  
这样我们就能够描述出她所认为的为 $\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]$ 的那个向量了。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/change-of-basis-matrix/TranslateHersToOursSetup.light.svg" />

This process, of scaling each of her basis vectors by the corresponding coordinates of $\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]$ and adding them together might feel familiar: It's matrix-vector multiplication, with a matrix whose columns represent Jennifer's basis vectors.  
将她的每个基向量分别乘以$\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 2\end{array}\right]$对应的坐标后再相加，  
这个过程可能看起来很熟悉：这就是矩阵-向量乘法，  
只不过这里的矩阵的列对应着詹妮弗的基向量。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/change-of-basis-matrix/TranslateHersToOursMatrix.light.svg" />

### How this is a transformation 为何这属于一种变换

In fact, once you understand matrix-vector multiplication as applying a certain linear transformation, say by watching what might be the most important video in this series, <LessonLink id="linear-transformations">chapter 3</LessonLink>, there's a pretty intuitive way to think about what's going on here.  
实际上，一旦你将矩阵-向量乘法理解为某种线性变换的应用——  
比如通过观看本系列中可能最重要的视频，  
即第3章——就能以一种相当直观的方式理解这里发生的事情。

A matrix whose columns represent Jennifer's basis vectors can be thought of as a transformation that moves our basis vectors, $\hat{\imath}$ and $\hat{\jmath}$, the things we think of when we say $\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]$ and $\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]$, to Jennifer's basis vectors, the things she thinks of when she says $\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]$ and $\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]$.  
一个其列向量代表詹妮弗的基向量的矩阵，可以被视为一种变换，  
它将我们的基向量 $\hat{\imath}$ 和 $\hat{\jmath}$ ——  
也就是我们提到 $\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]$和$\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\1 \end{array}\right]$ 时所想到的向量——  
转换为詹妮弗的基向量，即她提到 $\left[\begin{array}{c} 1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]$ 和 $\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\1 \end{array}\right]$ 时所想到的向量。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/change-of-basis-matrix/AlternativeBasisMatrix.light.svg" />

For example, let's walk through what it means to take the vector that we think of as having coordinates $\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]$, and applying this transformation. Before the linear transformation we're thinking of this vector as a certain linear combination of basis vectors, $-1\hat{\imath} + 2\hat{\jmath}$.  
例如，我们来具体看看如何对坐标为 $\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 2\end{array}\right]$ 的向量应用这种变换。  
在线性变换之前，我们把这个向量看作是基向量的某种线性组合，即 $-1\hat{\imath} + 2\hat{\jmath}$ 。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/change-of-basis-matrix/BeforeTransformation.light.svg">
  How we think of $\left[\begin{array} {c} -1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]$  
  我们如何理解 $\left[\begin{array} {c} -1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]$
</Figure>

The key feature of linear transformations is that the resulting vector will be that same linear combination of the new basis vectors, $-1 \text{ (the place where } \hat{\imath} \text{ lands) } + 2 \text{ (the place where } \hat{\jmath} \text{ lands)}$.  
线性变换的关键特征在于，所得向量仍是新基向量的相同线性组合，  
即$-1$（$\hat{\imath}$所在的位置）$+ 2$（$\hat{\jmath}$所在的位置）​。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/change-of-basis-matrix/AfterTransformation.light.svg">
  How Jennifer thinks of $\left[\begin{array}  {c} -1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]$  
  詹妮弗如何看待 $\left[\begin{array}  {c} -1 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]$
</Figure>

What this matrix does is transform our misconception of what Jennifer means into the actual vector that she's referring to.  
这个矩阵的作用是将我们对于詹妮弗所指含义的误解，转化为她实际所指的向量。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/change-of-basis-matrix/Result.light.svg" />

This can feel kind of backwards. Geometrically this matrix transforms our grid into Jennifer's grid, yet numerically it's translating a vector described in her language to our language.  
这看起来有点反直觉。  
从几何角度而言，该矩阵将我们的坐标系转换为詹妮弗的坐标系，  
但从数值上看，它则是将用她的语言描述的向量转换成我们的语言。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/change-of-basis-matrix/CanFeelBackwards.light.svg" />

One way to keep it straight, is to think about how it takes our misconception of what Jennifer means, the vector we get using the same coordinates in our own system, then transforms it into the vector that she really meant, but now expressed using our coordinate system.  
要理清这一点，可以这样想：  
它首先利用我们对自己对Jennifer意图的误解，用我们自身坐标系中的相同坐标得到一个向量，  
然后再将其转换成她真正想要的向量，只不过现在是用我们的坐标系来表示的。

### Going from ours to hers 从我们的到她的

What about if you want to go the other way around? In the example we used earlier in this chapter, we had a vector with coordinates $\left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]$ in our system.  
如果想反着来会怎么样呢？在本章前面举的例子中，我们的坐标系里有一个坐标为 $\left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]$ 的向量。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/change-of-basis-matrix/TranslateOursToHers.light.svg" />

How do we know that it would have coordinates $\left[\begin{array}{c} 5/3 \\ 1/3 \end{array}\right]$ in Jennifer's system?  
我们如何知道它在Jennifer的系统中会有坐标$\left[\begin{array}{c} 5/3 \\ 1/3 \end{array}\right]$？  

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/change-of-basis-matrix/TranslateOursToHersAlternative.light.svg" />

Well, you start with that change of basis matrix that translates Jennifer's language into ours, then you take its inverse. Remember, the inverse of a transformation is a new transformation that corresponds to playing that first one backwards.  
嗯，你首先使用那个将Jennifer的坐标系转换为我们坐标系的基变换矩阵，然后再取它的逆矩阵。  
记住，一个变换的逆变换就是另一个新的变换，相当于将原来的变换反向执行。


<Figure image="$lesson/figures/change-of-basis-matrix/InverseMatrix.light.svg" />

In this case, the inverse of the change of basis matrix that has Jennifer's basis as its columns ends up working out to have columns $\left[\begin{array}{c} 1/3 \\ -1/3 \end{array}\right]$, and $\left[\begin{array}{c} 1/3 \\ 2/3 \end{array}\right]$.  
在这种情况下，以Jennifer的基向量作为列的基变换矩阵的逆矩阵，  
其列向量最终为$\left[\begin{array}{c} 1/3 \\ -1/3 \end{array}\right]$和$\left[\begin{array}{c} 1/3 \\ 2/3 \end{array}\right]$。

$$
\left[\begin{array}{cc}
2 & -1 \\
1 & 1
\end{array}\right]^{-1}=\left[\begin{array}{cc}
1 / 3 & 1 / 3 \\
-1 / 3 & 2 / 3
\end{array}\right]
$$

So for example, to see what the vector $\left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]$ looks like in Jennifer's system, we multiply this inverse change of basis matrix by $\left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]$, which works out to be $\left[\begin{array}{c} 5/3 \\ 1/3 \end{array}\right]$.  
例如，要了解向量$\left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]$在Jennifer的坐标系中的样子，  
我们需要将这个逆基变换矩阵与$\left[\begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 2 \end{array}\right]$相乘，  
计算结果为$\left[\begin{array}{c} 5/3 \\ 1/3 \end{array}\right]$。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/change-of-basis-matrix/TranslateOursToHersMatrix.light.svg" />

In practice, especially when you're working in more than two dimensions, you'd use a computer to compute the matrix which represents this inverse, but for a small matrix, like $M = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix}$, the inverse can be computed by swapping along one diagonal, negating the other, and dividing by the determinant.  
在实践中，尤其是当处理超过二维的空间时，人们会使用计算机来计算表示该逆矩阵的矩阵；  
但对于像$M = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d\end{bmatrix}$这样的小型矩阵，  
其逆矩阵可以通过交换对角线上的元素、取另一对角线上元素的相反数，再除以行列式来计算。

$$
M ^{-1} =  \frac{1}{\det(M)} \begin{bmatrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{bmatrix}
$$

## Translating transformations  转换的翻译

So that, in a nutshell, is how to translate the description of individual vectors back and forth between coordinate systems: the matrix whose columns represent Jennifer's basis vectors written in our coordinate system translates vectors written in her language to vectors written in our language.  
简而言之，这就是如何在坐标系之间来回翻译对单个向量的描述:  
矩阵的列代表詹妮弗在我们的坐标系中写的基础向量，
将用她的语言写的向量翻译成用我们的语言写的。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/translating-transformations/FromHersToOurs.light.svg" />

The inverse of this matrix does the opposite, translating vectors written in our language to vectors written in her language.  
该矩阵的逆矩阵则起到相反的作用，将用我们的语言表示的向量转换为用她的语言表示的向量。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/translating-transformations/FromOursToHers.light.svg" />

However, vectors aren't the only thing we describe using coordinates. For this next part, it's important that you're comfortable representing transformations with matrices, and that you know how matrix multiplication corresponds to composing successive transformations. Definitely pause and take a look at <LessonLink id="linear-transformations">chapter 3</LessonLink> and <LessonLink id="matrix-multiplication">chapter 4</LessonLink> if any of this feels uneasy.  
不过，坐标并不仅仅用于描述向量。  
在接下来的内容中，你需要能够熟练地用矩阵表示变换，  
并且明白矩阵乘法如何对应于连续变换的复合。  
如果对此有任何疑问，务必暂停一下，重新阅读第3章和第4章。

### Follow Rotation   跟随轮换

Consider some linear transformation, like a $90$ degree counterclockwise rotation. In our coordinate system, we represent this with the matrix $\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{array}\right]$. The first column tells us that $\hat{\imath}$ goes to $\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]$, and the second column tells us that $\hat{\jmath}$ goes to $\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]$.  
考虑一些线性变换，比如90度逆时针旋转。  
在我们的坐标系中，我们用矩阵$\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{array}\right]$表示。  
第一列告诉我们，  $\hat{\imath}$ 会到  $\left[\begin{array}{c} 0 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]$，  
第二列告诉我们，  $\hat{\jmath}$ 会到  $\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 0 \end{array}\right]$ 。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/translating-transformations/RotationMatrixOurs.light.svg" />

This representation is heavily tied up in our choice of basis vectors from the fact that we're following $\hat{\imath}$ and $\hat{\jmath}$ in the first place, to the fact that we record their landing spots in our own coordinate system. How would Jennifer describe a $90$ degree rotation of space?  
这种表示与我们的基向量选择密切相关，从我们一开始就遵循 $\hat{\imath}$ 和 $\hat{\jmath}$ ，  
到我们在自己的坐标系中记录它们的着陆点。  
詹妮弗会如何描述 $90$ 度的空间旋转？
 
<Figure image="$lesson/figures/translating-transformations/RotationMatrixTheirs.light.svg" />

You might be tempted to just translate the columns of our rotation matrix into Jennifer's language, but this is not quite right. Those columns represent where our basis vectors $\hat{\imath}$ and $\hat{\jmath}$ go, but the matrix Jennifer wants should represent where her basis vectors land, and it needs to describe those landing spots in her language.  
你可能会想直接将旋转矩阵的各列用詹妮弗的语言来表达，但这样并不完全正确。  
那些列代表的是我们的基向量$\hat{\imath}$和$\hat{\jmath}$移动后的位置，  
而詹妮弗想要的矩阵应体现的是她自己的基向量最终所在的位置，  
并且需要用她的语言来描述这些位置。

### The process 过程

Here's a common way to think about how this is done: Start with any vector, written in Jennifer's language. Rather than trying to follow what happens to it in terms of her language, first translate it into our language using the change of basis matrix, the one whose columns represent her basis vectors in our language. This gives the same vector written in our language.  
这里有一个常见的思考方法:从任何用Jennifer语言编写的矢量开始。  
与其试图用她的语言来跟踪它发生的事情，  
不如先使用基矩阵的变化将其翻译成我们的语言，  
基矩阵的列代表她的基向量。  
这给出了用我们的语言编写的相同矢量。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/translating-transformations/TranslateMatrixChange.light.svg" />

Then apply the transformation matrix to it by multiplying on the left. This tells us where that vector lands, but in our language.  
然后通过左乘将其与变换矩阵相乘。  
这能告诉我们该向量最终落在何处，但是以我们的语言来表示的。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/translating-transformations/TranslateMatrixTransform.light.svg" />

As a last step, apply the inverse change of basis matrix, multiplied on the left as usual, to get this transformed vector in Jennifer's language.  
最后一步，像往常一样从左侧乘以逆基变换矩阵，  
从而得到用詹妮弗的语言表示的该变换后的向量。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/translating-transformations/TranslateMatrixInverse.light.svg" />

Since we could do this to any vector written in her language, first applying the change of basis matrix, then the transformation then the inverse change of basis, that composition of three matrices gives us the transformation matrix in Jennifer's language. It takes in a vector in her language, and spits out the transformed version of that vector in her language.  
       由于我们可以对用她的表述方式写出的任意向量都这样做：  
       先应用基变换矩阵，再进行变换，最后应用基逆变换，  
       这三者组合而成的矩阵就是用詹妮弗的语言表示的变换矩阵。  
       它输入一个用她语言表示的向量，输出该向量的变换后的版本，同样也用她的语言表示。

For this example, where Jennifer's basis vectors look like $\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]$ and $\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]$ to us, and we're translating a $90$ degree rotation, the product of these three matrices, if you work through it, has columns $\left[\begin{array}{c} 1/3 \\ 5/3 \end{array}\right]$ and $\left[\begin{array}{c} -2/3 \\ -1/3 \end{array}\right]$. So if Jennifer multiplies that matrix by the coordinates of a vector in her system, it will return the $90$ degree rotated version of her vector, expressed in her coordinate system.  
在这个例子中，詹妮弗的基向量在我们看来是$\left[\begin{array}{c} 2 \\ 1\end{array}\right]$和$\left[\begin{array}{c} -1 \\ 1 \end{array}\right]$，而我们要实现的是90度旋转。  
计算这三个矩阵的乘积后，其列分别为$\left[\begin{array}{c} 1/3 \\ 5/3 \end{array}\right]$和$\left[\begin{array}       {c} -2/3 \\ -1/3 \end{array}\right]$。  
因此，如果詹妮弗用该矩阵乘以她所在坐标系中向量的坐标，  
就能得到该向量在90度旋转后的结果，且仍以她的坐标系表示。

<Figure image="$lesson/figures/translating-transformations/TranslateMatrix.light.svg" />

In general, whenever you see an expression like $A^{-1}MA$, it suggests a mathematical sort of empathy. The middle matrix represents a transformation as you see it, the outer two matrices represent the empathy, this shift in perspective, and the full matrix product represents that same transformation as someone else sees it.  
一般来说，每当看到诸如 $A^{-1}MA$ 这样的表达式时，它就体现了一种数学上的“共情”​。  
中间的矩阵代表你所看到的变换，外侧的两个矩阵代表这种共情，即视角的转换，  
而整个矩阵乘积则代表另一个人所看到的同一变换。
